Magnet Forensics

Waterloo, Ontario

I worked on the Ignite team responsible for the Nexus product. This is a well established product team so many of my tasks were small features or bug fixes. I gained a good amount of exposure to C#, ASP.NET, React, TypeScript, and AWS. I am now pretty familiar with AWS DynamoDB and CloudWatch. Magnet Forensics

About the company

  • In the digital forensics industry
  • Develops software to assist law enforcement in collecting digital evidence
  • Recently acquired by US private equity firm Thoma Bravo
  • Located in Waterloo
  • Fully remote work policy
  • Almost all of the people on my team are UofG alumni

What I did

I was on a team that develops the web application for Magnet Nexus. Nexus is used by companies and government agencies to collect data on company owned employee devices. It's commonly used when a breach occurs or an employee is suspected to be mishandling proprietary company data. There are some very well known companies using the product. There are a few other teams that handle certain parts of the product. This is one of the smaller products and is fairly new. Magnet has older cash cows primarily used by law enforcement and governments.

Since the product is well established and has paying customers I worked on a lot of bug fixes and small features. The backend was written in C# and ASP.NET and used DynamoDB and S3 for data storage, no SQL databases. The frontend was written in React and TypeScript. Near the end of my term it was updated to be using Material UI to align with other products the company has. I spent a while poking around the backend and CloudWatch to fix bugs as well as making some changes to a few DynamoDB tables. I did a few tickets with React and TS also fixing bugs and doing some refactoring work.

This was definitely a different experience then my previous terms, but I enjoyed it. While not as fast paced as other companies it was a good introduction to the development process at large companies. I feel like I gained solid soft skills from communicating with everyone on the team and adjusting to a remote work environment. I did also gain some more technical skills especially in AWS. It was a fulfilling term and I had a great time.

The office

My Learning Goals

Gain experience with C# and ASP.NET

I wanted to learn C# and ASP.NET since that's what the backend is written in. My measure of success was completing at least 5 backend focused tickets.

I've completed more then 5 tickets that required backend changes. Some tickets required a good amount of new feature work where I learned a lot about C# and ASP.NET. I still gained a good amount of experience with C# by working on bug fixes in the backend. Those tickets were tricky and required a lot of poking around and understanding existing logic.

Gain more experience with TypeScript

TypeScript is popular so I wanted to have some professional experience with it. My measure of success was completing at least 3 frontend focused tickets.

I've completed a lot more then 3 tickets. I worked on bug fixes and some component refactoring work so I definitely gained experience with TypeScript

Learn more about the agile process

The team follows the agile process so I wanted to learn it since it's very common in big tech companies. My measure of success was being able to define 5 terms and participate in 2 ceremonies regularly. If possible be the facilitator for a sprint.

I've learned a little bit more then 5 agile terms and I participated in more then 2 ceremonies regularly. Every day there was standup and every week there would be some team ceremony that I participated in. I was also the facilitator of all the agile/ceremony stuff for 2 weeks (a sprint).

Conclusion

I accomplished all of my learning goals and enjoyed my term. I had a great time on the Ignite team. Everyone on that team is amazing and super helpful.
